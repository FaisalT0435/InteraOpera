# Meridian Fixed Income Fund — Investment Guidelines

Document reference: MFIF-IG-2025-03. This document states the binding investment guidelines of the Meridian Fixed Income Fund and prevails over any summary material. Terms not defined here carry the meaning given in the fund's constitutive documents.

## 1. Fund overview

The Meridian Fixed Income Fund invests primarily in Singapore-dollar-denominated investment grade debt with selective allocations to higher-yielding credit. The base currency of the Meridian Fixed Income Fund is the Singapore dollar (SGD). All limits in this document are expressed against the fund's net asset value unless stated otherwise.

The fund is offered to accredited and institutional investors. The fund is supervised by the Monetary Authority of Singapore (MAS). The investment manager maintains the compliance programme described in Section 6 to meet its regulatory obligations.

## 2. Eligible investments and allocation limits

The fund may hold government securities, investment grade corporate bonds, high yield corporate bonds, structured credit instruments, and cash or cash equivalents. Derivative use is restricted to hedging.

Allocation limits are hard limits, monitored at market value:

- Investment grade corporate bonds must make up at least 40% of the portfolio. This floor anchors the fund's credit profile and applies after settlement of pending trades.
- High yield bonds are capped at 15% of the fund's net asset value. For the purpose of this cap, "high yield" means any instrument rated below BBB- by at least two recognised rating agencies.
- Structured credit is capped at 10% of the fund's net asset value. Eligible structured credit is limited to senior tranches with observable pricing.
- Aggregate non-investment-grade exposure is capped at 20% of net asset value. This aggregate combines high yield bonds, non-rated instruments, and any structured credit tranche rated below investment grade, so the individual caps above cannot be stacked to exceed it.

## 3. Concentration limits

Concentration is measured across all instrument types at the issuer and issuer-group level, using the fund's issuer hierarchy reference data.

Exposure to any single issuer is capped at 5% of net asset value. Aggregate exposure to any issuer group is capped at 10% of net asset value. Government and quasi-government issuers explicitly guaranteed by the Republic of Singapore are exempt from both caps.

## 4. Risk metrics and limits

The portfolio is managed against interest-rate and credit-quality limits. Portfolio modified duration must stay between 2.0 and 6.5 years. Duration is computed on a market-value-weighted basis across all interest-rate-sensitive holdings, including hedges.

The portfolio must maintain a weighted average credit rating of BBB or better. Where agencies disagree, the second-best rating is used. All risk metrics are monitored daily. Monitoring output is retained with the compliance records described in the fund's operations manual.

## 5. Currency and liquidity

Non-SGD exposure may be held only where it improves the portfolio's risk-adjusted return and is hedged in the ordinary course. Unhedged non-SGD currency exposure is capped at 15% of net asset value.

To meet redemptions without forced selling, the fund maintains a liquidity buffer. At least 10% of the portfolio must be held in cash or Singapore Treasury bills. The buffer is measured daily at the same time as the risk metrics in Section 4.

## 6. Breach management

A breach of any limit in this document is a compliance event regardless of cause (active breach or passive breach through market movement). The Chief Risk Officer owns breach escalation. Passive breaches must be cured within ten business days unless the Chief Risk Officer approves a documented extension.

For breaches of the modified duration limit in Section 4, the escalation path is stricter: The Chief Investment Officer must be notified within one business day and a remediation plan filed within five. Remediation plans are tabled at the next risk committee meeting and tracked to closure.
