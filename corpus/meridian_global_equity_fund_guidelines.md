# Meridian Global Equity Fund — Investment Guidelines

Document reference: MGEF-IG-2025-02. This document states the binding investment guidelines of the Meridian Global Equity Fund, a separate fund in the Meridian range. It follows the same structure as the fixed income guidelines but its limits are materially different — do not apply one fund's limits to the other.

## 1. Fund overview

The Meridian Global Equity Fund invests in listed global equities with a quality-growth bias. The base currency of the Meridian Global Equity Fund is the US dollar (USD). All limits are expressed against net asset value unless stated otherwise.

The Meridian Global Equity Fund is likewise supervised by the Monetary Authority of Singapore as part of the Meridian fund range. The fund was launched in 2015 and tracks the Global Developed Equity Benchmark.

## 2. Eligible investments and allocation limits

The fund holds listed equities, depositary receipts, and cash. Fixed income instruments, including high yield bonds, are not eligible investments for this fund; any reference to credit limits in the Meridian range applies to the fixed income fund only.

Cash and cash equivalents are capped at 10% of net asset value under normal market conditions. A minimum of 90% of the portfolio must be invested in eligible equities measured at market value.

## 3. Concentration limits

For this fund, exposure to any single issuer is capped at 8% of net asset value, and aggregate exposure to any issuer group is capped at 16% of net asset value. Index heavyweights may be held up to benchmark weight plus 2%, whichever is greater.

Sector concentration is capped at benchmark weight plus 10 percentage points per GICS sector.

## 4. Risk metrics and limits

Equity risk is managed through tracking error and beta limits rather than duration. Ex-ante tracking error must stay between 2% and 6% annualised, and portfolio beta must remain between 0.8 and 1.2 against the benchmark.

Risk metrics for this fund are monitored weekly, with a full factor decomposition produced monthly. Breaches of the tracking error band are escalated to the Head of Equities, who must be notified within two business days.

## 5. Currency and liquidity

Currency exposure follows the benchmark's currency composition. Unhedged non-USD currency exposure is capped at 40% of net asset value for this fund, reflecting the global mandate.

At least 2% of the portfolio must be held in cash for settlement purposes. The fund's net asset value is published weekly on each dealing day.
